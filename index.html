SightLife Global Scorecard

A global scorecard for SightLife to keep track of progress made tied to each department (Advocacy & Policy, Clinical Training, Prevention, and Eye Bank Development) with a mission to eradicate Corneal Blindness by 2040.

The Problem

Right now, in this instance, there are more than 12.7 million people in the world that are needlessly suffering from corneal blindness. Corneal blindness is the scarring of the cornea caused by an injury or disease. Damage to the cornea is more common than we think, but with SightLife, all of the 12.7 million people suffering may all be relieved by 2040. SightLifeâ€™s main goal is to eliminate this life-damaging ailment.

But with their current system of organizing/visualizing data, it is a hindrance to their overall goal. This is why the global scorecard is a must-have. With this scorecard, it allows regional leads to have a platform where they can keep track of progress made tied to each region around the world where they are currently working to eradicate corneal blindness. This scorecard will provide visualizations that show trends over time, address different success metrics, and contain customizable dashboards for users from various departments
